Quick check:

Ionospheric delay

The ionosphere is a charged plasma that slows GNSS radio waves. Extra path delay shifts the computed range, moving the guidance line.

Key properties

  • Delay ‚àù TEC (total electrons along the path).
  • Frequency dependent: L1 affected more than L2/L5.
  • Mitigation: dual-frequency combination, RTK/PPP corrections.

What to notice in the sim

Raise Ionospheric Activity. Drift grows. Switch to Dual or RTK. Error circle shrinks. Increase Speed. Tracking worsens slightly.

Dilution of Precision (PDOP) & Scintillation (S4)

PDOP summarizes satellite geometry. Low PDOP means satellites are well spread. S4 indicates signal amplitude fluctuation from ionospheric turbulence.

Key properties

  • More constellations ‚Üì PDOP on average.

Did you know? TEC usually peaks near local noon and after sunset. During solar storms, the whole curve lifts.

Quick check:

TEC & diurnal pattern

TEC counts electrons along a GNSS path. Solar radiation and geometry create daily peaks that raise positioning error when uncorrected.

Key properties

  • Two peaks common: around noon and post-sunset.
  • Storms add a baseline lift across hours.
  • Better receivers scale the delay down strongly.

What to notice in the sim

Increase Storm Lift. Entire heatmap shifts to warmer colors. Switch Receiver to RTK. Colors cool down.
