Simulator 1 — Active regions, loops, and flare bursts

Complexity: beta-γ · Shear: 0.55 · Flare prob: 18%/min · Last: C5.4 active
Concept guide: Active regions → shear → flare likelihood
  • Definition. An active region is a solar area with strong magnetic fields. Hale class (α, β, βγ, βγδ) encodes magnetic complexity.
  • Properties. Higher complexity and larger shear/twist increase magnetic free energy and flare probability.
  • Observations. Bright coronal loops arch between opposite polarities. Flares appear as impulsive brightenings and particle ejections.
  • How to use. Set class to βγ or βγδ and raise shear. Watch flare probability and triggered bursts.

Simulator 2 — X-ray light curve & class thresholds

Peak: M2.3 · Rise 6 min · Decay 18 min
Concept guide: GOES classes and flare shapes
  • Definition. GOES soft X-ray flux in W/m² defines classes: C=10⁻⁶, M=10⁻⁵, X=10⁻⁴ baseline, with decimal multipliers.
  • Properties. Light curves show rise and decay. Peak class maps to the curve maximum.
  • Observations. Higher background shifts the whole curve upward and can hide small events.
  • How to use. Choose class and duration, inject a flare, read peak and times.

Simulator 3 — CME shock travel & ETA

Speed: 1100 km/s · Width: 70° · ETA: 31 h geoeffective?
Concept guide: CME geometry and geoeffectiveness
  • Definition. A CME is a large ejection of plasma and magnetic field from the Sun.
  • Properties. Speed sets ETA. Width and launch longitude set whether Earth sits inside the cone.
  • Observations. Disk-center launches with wide cones are most likely to hit Earth.
  • How to use. Adjust speed, width, and longitude. Watch ETA and the geoeffective flag.

Simulator 4 — R-scale radio blackout on the sunlit side

Class: M5.0 · R3 · HF degraded over daylight hemisphere operational impact
Concept guide: R-scale and daylight geometry
  • Definition. The R-scale (R1–R5) rates HF radio blackout severity from flare X-rays on the daylit hemisphere.
  • Properties. Higher class and multiplier raise R. Effects follow the subsolar point across Earth’s rotation.
  • Observations. R3+ degrades HF and affects aviation comms and GNSS accuracy on the sunlit side.
  • How to use. Set class and multiplier, click the map to move the subsolar point, observe day/night split.
